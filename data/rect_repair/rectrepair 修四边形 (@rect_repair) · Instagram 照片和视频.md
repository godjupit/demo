[![THANK YOU to everyone for participating in the first Found Media VJ Battle Royale (既有媒介VJ大战) last weekend!!

We split the entire venue in half, where 24 VJs, divided into 2 teams, went head to head showcasing their VJ skills using publicly available videos only. Each round lasted 10 minutes, and the audience voted by physically standing (dancing) in front of the VJ they enjoyed more!!!

The videos played were… all over the map. news broadcasts, demoscene videos, Chainsaw Man, old computer graphics, Hatsune Miku, Code Geass, math and science lectures, Dreamcast, Nintendo 64, FF7, bubble sort, high-speed dogfighting, toasts falling over, Empresses in the Palace, Street Fighters, bilibili memes, 还珠格格,华强买瓜, 郭德纲... the list goes on and on

Because of the “browser only” rule, everyone got incredibly creative with their setups. Some techniques included: MIDI controlled browser plugins, turntable-scratch simulator plugins, custom AppleScript controlling browser window animations, DV camera feedback, TouchDesigner with browser, livecoding with Hydra, macOS’s built-in zoom feature, humble YouTube keyboard shortcuts, custom web sequencers that loop video to the beat, all kinds of hand-coded and vibe-coded plugins… etc etc.

[ ▶︎ ] Battle Format:
- There are 24 contestants in total for this arena. All participants will be divided equally into two teams, Red and Blue, to compete for team points.
- each round lasts 10 minutes.
- both the venue and the entire screen will be split in two.
- audience holds the power to decide the winner. During the final 10 seconds of each round, audience members must vote by physically moving to the left or right side of the room to support the Red or Blue team.
- after each round, the current 2 contestants will exit, and the next 2 will take the stage.
-  battle will last for 12 rounds. The team with the highest cumulative points wins.

[ ▶︎ ]  Rules:
- may only use public, existing videos from video websites or streaming platforms for VJ materials.
- must use browser to VJ.
- Wi-Fi and HDMI connections will be provided.
- highly encourage using "found" footage from the depths of the internet

We are inviting game creators and players from around the world to design and play [[alternative reality]] games during [[alternative reality]] WEEK! — a series of game jam and play events taking place over two weekends, dedicated to playful experiences that spill out of the screen and into the real world.

“Wait, do you mean Augmented Reality (AR)? “
Nope!

“Do you mean Alternate Reality Game (ARG)?”
..Well, kind of! 
Alternative, as in alternative games, alternative tech, alternative media, alternative infrastructure: another, perhaps more radical way of engaging with what’s already here. We are inspired by practices including alternate reality games, pervasive games, immersive theatre, performance art, public interventions, net art, urban Games, LARPs, etc., but we’re not precious about genre.

The goal is to design playful experiences that combine game mechanics with the real world. No-tech, low-tech, or high-tech approaches all welcome.

Based in Shanghai, [[rect*]]repair is an art–game collective building experimental games, alternative game jams and public events that blur play, fiction and community. Mostly self-organised, always collaborative, their work lives in the gaps between digital and physical worlds.

With the fund, they’ll launch a global ARG game jam, connecting independent art and game spaces across borders. Powered by low-tech and alternative infrastructures: NFC tags, urban fixtures, local networks, ham radios, the platform promotes play that creates friction, intimacy and site-specific meaning.

Led by @jyolyyyu and @v10101a , [[rect*]]repair see ARGs as a vital, overlooked form of play: real people, building worlds together, face to face — at a moment when that kind of connection feels more urgent than ever.
